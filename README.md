# DekutHack-App
An IoT sensor-driven app monitoring air quality in real-time. Track CO, NO2, VOC, and more. Visualize data with charts for informed environmental decisions.

### Project Overview: Enhancing Air Quality Monitoring with Satellite Imagery

#### Project Title:
Integrating Satellite Imagery with IoT-Based Air Quality Monitoring for Precise Pollution Source Identification

#### Executive Summary:
Our project revolutionizes air quality monitoring by combining IoT-based sensors with advanced satellite imagery. This innovative approach enables real-time detection of pollutants and precise identification of their sources, leading to more effective pollution management and policy-making. The system includes real-time alerts and notifications, a user-friendly data visualization platform, and collaboration with NEMA (National Environment Management Authority) to ensure regulatory compliance.

#### Objectives:
1. *Developed an IoT-based air quality monitoring device* equipped with sensors to detect pollutants such as NO2, CO, Ethyl, and VOC.
2. *Integrated satellite imagery* to pinpoint pollution sources.
3. *Created a data processing and visualization platform* for real-time air quality data and pollution source locations.
4. *Implemented pollution reduction strategies* based on collected data.
5. *Tested and validated the prototype* in a real-world environment.
6. *Collaborated with NEMA* for regulatory compliance and support.

#### Alignment with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):
1. *<b>Goal 3:</b> Good Health and Well-being*
2. *<b>Goal 11:</b> Sustainable Cities and Communities*
3. *<b>Goal 13:</b> Climate Action*

#### Background and Rationale:
Air pollution is a critical issue causing health problems and environmental degradation. Traditional air quality monitoring systems lack the ability to identify exact pollution sources. By integrating satellite imagery, our project provides a comprehensive solution that monitors air quality and identifies pollution sources, enabling targeted interventions.

#### Methodology:
1. *<b>IoT-Based Device</b>*: Sensors for NO2, CO, Ethyl, and VOC; ESP32 microcontroller for data transmission; Africa's Talking API for alerts.
2. *<b>Satellite Imagery</b>*: Google Earth Engine Sentinel-5P data; machine learning for image analysis.
3. *<b>Data Platform</b>*: Firebase Realtime Database for data storage; Android app for visualization; GIS tools for mapping.
4. *<b>Deployment Strategy</b>*: Assess new areas using satellite imagery; place IoT devices in identified hotspots for real-time data; automated alerts and notifications to authorities and public.
5. *<b>Pollution Reduction</b>*: Real-time alerts, targeted interventions, community engagement, policy recommendations.
6. *<b>Testing and Validation</b>*: Field deployment, data analysis, stakeholder feedback.

#### Achievements and Outcomes:
- Functional prototype of an integrated air quality monitoring system.
- Improved accuracy in detecting and pinpointing pollution sources.
- User-friendly dashboard for real-time monitoring.
- Enhanced decision-making for policymakers and environmental agencies.
- Effective pollution reduction strategies implemented.
- Support for NEMA’s environmental management efforts.

4. *<b>SDG Impact</b>*: Contribution to SDGs.

Our project combines IoT and satellite imagery to enhance air quality monitoring. It supports effective environmental management and policy-making, aligns with SDGs, and demonstrates significant potential at the MLSA hackathon competition. Additionally, it aids NEMA in improving environmental quality and public health.
